Qurt: A Precious Stone

A Kazakh "cheese of resilience" saved the lives of imprisoned women at the biggest female labor camp of the USSR

By Nuri Atymtay

Gertrude Platais, former prisoner:

Oh, Lord, this is not a stone.
It smells so much of milk.
And the flame of hope fluttered in my soul,
And a lump arose in my throat.
This is why women risked their children!
They protected us from illness,
They saved us from unbelief.
They understood that we were not enemies,
But just unfortunate women.
And they helped as much as they could,
Amazed us with their humanity.
I silently crawled across the ice,
Collecting precious stones.
And at night in the coldest barracks,
On the ground desecrated by executioners,
I, a German, prayed to the Muslim God,
But I did not ask for anything for myself.
I asked for health for the old people,
For women-mothers - happiness.
